Who I Am

Hello, I’m Kris Yotam, a passionate classical art apprentice. My journey began when I was fascinated by the intricate techniques of traditional painting and drawing. I have dedicated myself to honing the skills passed down through generations of masters in the art world. This page documents my personal and artistic development as I continue to explore the beauty of classical art.

My Influences

Throughout my artistic journey, I have been influenced by a variety of great artists who have shaped my understanding and appreciation of classical techniques. Some of my primary influences include:

  • Michelangelo - For his mastery of the human form and his exceptional use of light and shadow.
  • Rembrandt - For his deep emotional expression and expert use of chiaroscuro.
  • Leonardo da Vinci - For his relentless pursuit of knowledge and innovative techniques.
  • John Singer Sargent - For his brilliant portrayal of light and texture, especially in portraiture.

My Mentor: Sadie Valerie

My artistic growth has been deeply influenced by my mentor, Sadie Valerie, a respected artist in the classical community. Sadie has been a guiding force in my artistic development, providing invaluable insight into technique, composition, and the philosophy behind classical art. Her attention to detail and passion for preserving traditional methods have been a constant inspiration to me. I also take after Juliette Aristedes as I train with her several books.

My Artistic Journey

2024

Started Classical Art Apprenticeship

I began my apprenticeship with Sadie Valerie, focusing on classical techniques such as drawing from life and studying old masters.

2025

First Solo Exhibition

My first solo exhibition was held, showcasing my portraits and studies in classical oil painting techniques.

2026

Exploration of Watercolor

Incorporated watercolor into my practice, experimenting with blending classical techniques into this fluid medium.

2027

Focus on Landscape Painting

Shifted focus to capturing natural landscapes, integrating classical principles of composition and light into the work.

2028

Expanding My Artistic Horizons

I’m currently exploring new themes and styles, constantly pushing the boundaries of my classical training.
